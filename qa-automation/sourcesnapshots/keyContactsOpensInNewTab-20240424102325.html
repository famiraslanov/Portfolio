Summary

The Colorado Fire & Police Pension Association (FPPA) was established in January 1980 as a defined benefit pension plan for the police officers and firefighters in the state of Colorado, US.

The system also sponsors the Colorado Fire & Police Pension Association Self-Directed defined contribution plans.

The investment approach is led by CIO Scott Simon who has served in the position since 2007. Prior to this, Simon worked at the University of Colorado Foundation, focusing on alternative investments. He also spent time at CenturyLink Asset Management as a portfolio manager and at Denver Employees Retirement Plan as an investment analyst.

Investment portfolio

Overview

  • The 8.6% hedge funds allocation represents an diversifiers portfolio, formerly absolute return, and the 13.8% fixed income allocation contains a 9.3% rates allocation and a 4.5% credit allocation.
  • The fund is comprised of three asset pools; the long-term pool represents 82.9% of the portfolio, the glide-path pool 16.9%, and the short-term pool 0.2%. 
  • The long-term pool returned 9.81% for the 1-year period, against a policy benchmark of 10.87%.
  • Within its alternatives allocation, the total fund allocates 22.6% to private capital, 3.4% to real assets, and 4% to real estate.
